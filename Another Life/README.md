In another life I was a Jew in a concentration camp. It was more
horrible than you can imagine and, in the end, I was burned alive. I was
sent to Burchenwhul in a jack-booted raid in the middle of one swampy
night. Both me and the love of my life were snatched from our beds and
eventually forced onto a train to the death camp. After living a loving
life, it ended in the bottom of a furnace in excruciating pain.

But that was a lifetime ago. I don’t remember these things, but they
happened nonetheless. The experience burned into my soul. In a way, this
kind of experience ends up informing the rest of your existence. This
time around things are a bit different, but life always seems to find a
way to resonate with the unconscious. For example, the guards that made
the end of my last life a living hell are now my best friends.

Of course, I didn’t know who they were in that other life and, as young
children do, I made friends with those whose personalities meshed with
mine. They also had no way of knowing the seemingly unending torture
they inflicted to an entire camp of desperate Jews, but I imagine that
experience is also burned into their souls as well. The universe’s idea
of divine retribution I guess.

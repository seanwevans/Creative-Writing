-   Historical Figure: Satan

    -   Narrative: The adversary gives the demon a magical poem and
        casts the demon into spacetime. the demon exists outside of time
        and space as a formless mass. Satan and the demon are “the same”
        but different. They must “separate” in order to accomplish a
        task within spacetime. Satan and the demon are communicating
        somehow. Everything is vague and in the wrong tense. The unnamed
        blob remains outside of spacetime and formless, while the demon

Chapter 1

-   Historical Figure: The caveman om (c. 30,000 bce)

-   Narrative: the demon, after chilling for a couple of billion years,
    decides to kill itself. Before he does, he observes the caveman
    called ‘om’ waste resources to cultivate a beautiful flower. Others
    around him are struggling to survive their brutal existence and he
    is just doing everything he can to make this pretty flower grow. The
    demon, seeing this, gifts om with ambition and decides to give it a
    couple of thousand more years to see what happens. This event
    eventually ends up kicking off the agricultural revolution and the
    rise of human civilization. Om eventually dies and the demon decides
    to carry on his work disguised as “different om’s” throughout
    history, partially as a tribute to om and his wacky garden, and
    partially to inflict a more direct pressure on humanity. This is the
    switch that turns him from disinterested in humanity to interested.
    The demon doesn’t invent any of the deadly sins, he just uses them
    as a force multiplier to really crank up the consequences of extreme
    action to a civilization level.

Chapter 2: The Dawn of Agriculture and the Birth of Gluttony

-   Historical Figure: om’s great-grandson (29,900 bce)

-   Narrative:  As om’s great-grandson, the demon easily becomes an
    influential leader in his Neolithic community. The demon, in this
    guise, introduces advanced agricultural techniques, leading to
    abundance. This abundance, however, also leads to gluttony, altering
    the community's relationship with food and consumption.

Chapter 3: The Age of Empires and Pride

-   Historical Figure: Imhotep (late 27th century BC)

-   Narrative: In this role, the demon inspires monumental architecture
    and the expansion of empires, fostering a sense of pride that often
    crosses into hubris and the downfall of great civilizations.

Chapter 4: The Foundation of Religions and Envy

-   Historical Figure: Confucius (c. 551 – c. 479 BCE).

-   Narrative: Here, the demon influences the foundation of a major
    religion. While this brings people together, it also inadvertently
    seeds envy among different factions and sects, leading to conflicts
    and schisms.

Chapter 5: The Era of Conquests and Wrath

-   Historical Figure: Alexander the Great (July 356 BC – 11 June 323
    BC)

-   Narrative: The demon, as a great conqueror, spreads influence
    through military might, but this also leads to widespread wrath and
    violence, showing the destructive side of ambition and power.

Chapter 6: The Rise of Currency and Greed

-   Historical Figure: Mansa Musa (c. 1312 – c. 1337).

-   Narrative: As this figure, the demon promotes the use of currency,
    revolutionizing trade, and economy. However, this innovation also
    sows the seeds of greed, as people begin to equate wealth with power
    and success.

Chapter 7: The Renaissance and Lust

-   Historical Figure: Leonardo Da Vinci (15 April 1452 – 2 May 1519).

-   Narrative: As a patron or creator of art during the Renaissance, the
    demon encourages the celebration of human form and passion, which
    inadvertently contributes to an era where lust and desire begin to
    overshadow more spiritual pursuits. Demon tries to be “good” but
    ultimately fails.

Chapter 8: The Industrial Revolution and Sloth

-   Historical Figure: Henry Ford (July 30, 1863 – April 7, 1947).

-   Narrative: The demon, as a key figure in the Industrial Revolution,
    ushers in technological advancements that greatly improve efficiency
    and productivity. However, this also leads to an unexpected
    consequence: a culture of sloth, where mechanization starts to erode
    the value of human labor and effort. As a result the holocaust
    happens.

Chapter 9: The Modern World and the Culmination of Sin

-   Historical Figure: Robin Williams (July 21, 1951 – August 11, 2014).

-   Narrative: In the present day, the demon reflects on how each of
    these advancements, while contributing to human progress, has also
    led to the manifestation of deadly sins. This chapter ties together
    the previous stories, showing the interconnectedness of these
    developments and their consequences.

Chapter 10: The death of the demon

-   Historical Figure: The net-runner champion Moe-Moe (HgF 23 2555 –
    LiK 888 2999).

-   Narrative: The culmination of the thousands of years of waiting. The
    demon is frustrated and desperate. Even though its works have echoed
    throughout humanity’s history things have never been better for
    them! Grasping for the last traps of desperation and exerting any
    control possible, the demon self-terminates. It dies frustrated
    inside without ever learning the “Truth”. After billions of years
    this was the straw that broke his back.

Chapter 11: The Cosmic Revelation

-   Historical Figure: Almighty gawd (α – Ω).

-   Narrative: The twist reveals the true origin of the demon's
    directive—the Lovecraftian deity known as “gawd”. This revelation
    puts the entire course of human history in a new perspective,
    highlighting the insignificance of human morality in the face of the
    being’s incomprehensible cosmic benevolence.

Chapter 12: The Cosmicer Revelation

-   Historical Figure: A mental patient in an asylum (1986 – 2023)

-   Narrative: It is revealed that the demon is actually a mentally ill
    human being living their life trapped in a mental institution. Its
    ambiguous if the patient is actually mentally ill or coping with
    imprisonment.

Chapter 13:  The Cosmicist Revelation

-   Historical Figure: Erf (14.8 billion BCE – Today)

-   Narrative: “everyone” is a “demon” “controlled” by gawd. gawd exists
    and this is his method of controlling people. The intersection of
    gawd’s power and spacetime becomes the slice of reality it wants you
    to see in order to make you do specific things. gawd “tricks” you
    into doing its bidding in the same way a parent tricks their child
    into learning.

Epilogue: Is the reader a part of the book?

-   Historical Figure: The Universe (Infinite)

-   Narrative: gawd is not so benevolent. Some other entity is doing to
    gawd what he is doing to us, and so on and so on in a great
    recursive, universal web. Just like the human, gawd itself is being
    played by some other even more incomprehensible entity.

Don’t They Know I’m Him?

By Sean Evans

Prologue

the demon

I'm him. I’ve been him. I will continue to be him.

In the time before time, the great mass of emulsified chaos congeals.
the demon exists in a formless miasma as it rests before the emergence
of spacetime. It was lonely but it wasn’t alone. From deep within
itself, a message arises:

Go, bring disorder; Take this boon.

The great blob, budding off a significant piece of itself like some kind
of fucked up mollusk, then casts the demon out into the beginning of
spacetime. A couple of billion years pass as we zoom in to a specific
arm of a certain spiral galaxy close into a speck of rusty iron dust
that is very slowly revolving around a tiny point of light. the demon
has made his home, and he lives quite fruitfully. Like a vampire who
feeds on order, he takes every opportunity to sow disharmony and reap
chaos. Like flotsam, it drifts.

Recently, neoliths have gone to taking to caves as residences. This
jaunt of intelligence is not unnoticed by the demon, but, at least
initially, it’s far beneath the threshold of reaction. The flow that had
been gifted by the blob seemed to be a special set of instructions. It
looked just like a missive directive. A fait accompli. What else could
it be?

Convinced the mystical infinite poem is directing his actions, it uses
the flow as its primary tool to sow chaos. Reciting it holds animals in
place and it imbues a deep, sleep-like hypnosis, in which the demon
gains total control over any living being for a limited time. Running
entire herds of buffalo off cliffs was a fun pastime for a couple of
hundred years.

Chapter 1

om

I need more Sequoia Banshee Boogers!

om sat transfixed at the beautiful flower while a light rain pittered
across his large, protruding forehead. Never, in his entire life, had he
seen anything like this flower. He couldn’t possibly put its beauty into
any kind of language. A delicate purple from one angle, a brilliant gold
from another, the freshly bloomed flower was ravenously beautiful. It
was an assault of beauty.

The decaying carcass om was dragging wasn’t nearly as beautiful, but om
seemed not to mind. The flower was everything now. It was the only thing
ever. What had he even been doing before this? Did it matter? It was
beautiful. om thought it even sounded nice. The small drops of water
softly slapping against the delicate petals was like music to the deaf.
There was a soft hum that seemed to emanate from the roots of the plant.

As he studied the amazing flora in more detail, om saw more and more
intricacies within the complex structures of its silken petals. It was
amazing. How could something this small be so precise? There were tiny
dark specks arrayed in a convoluted pattern that was simply
incomprehensible. He couldn’t believe the sweet smells that assailed him
either! This alien perfume was coming directly from the plant’s
interior, and it seemed almost visible, as dust projecting a haze, into
the muted morning light. om couldn’t look away. He was fascinated.

om never made it back to his shelter that day. Instead, he sat right
there, next to his flower, in the thin drizzle, and enjoyed the carcass
he had been dragging raw. As he ate, he stared through the flower. He
gaped at its petals and its stem. He was agog with each leaf. He
carefully noted each individual joint where the stem split. He stared
straight through the plant into its fleshy interior. om sniffed it so
often while he ate that the taste of rotten flesh, which was already a
non-concern, was somehow even further removed from consideration. His
fascination had quickly turned to obsession.

After a week planted next to the amazing flower, he becomes
ultra-protective of the flower. Not that anyone comes near him, but he
cleared space around the orchid, like an altar, and he started to build
what looks like a rudimentary earthen hut around the flower. The rotten
carcass, long eaten, would be mana right now. He must go off and
scavenge or he won’t be able to protect the flower. For the first time
in a week om goes off to scavenge so he doesn’t die.

Around day 3 of om’s flower vigil, the demon, fresh off killing a large
group of deer with deer aids, happened to stumble across om’s
semi-permanent structure. What is that? Isn’t that a flower? A common
orchid? What is that monkey doing? He, too, became obsessed; billions of
years ago now, but obsession doesn’t have a statute of limitations. From
the shadows, he watched om care for this ordinary, plain plant.
Sacrificing everything that would otherwise keep him alive to make sure
this insignificant plant can thrive.
